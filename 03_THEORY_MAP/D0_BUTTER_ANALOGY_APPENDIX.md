# Appendix — Butter Analogy for Self-Substrate Trace

Imagine light as a piece of butter. It does not move through an empty vacuum, because in D0 there is no external vacuum. It moves only by turning part of itself into the track by which its motion can later be recognized.

At every tick, some of the butter continues as the active piece, and some of it becomes trace. The trace is not somewhere else. It is the same substrate, reorganized as memory. From inside the process, the trace expands while the active piece contracts, although the total substrate is conserved.

This appendix is explanatory only. Its formal content is the retained/traced split \(P_N+Q_N=I\) and the feedback-return operator \(F_N=P_NU_N^\dagger Q_NU_NP_N\).
