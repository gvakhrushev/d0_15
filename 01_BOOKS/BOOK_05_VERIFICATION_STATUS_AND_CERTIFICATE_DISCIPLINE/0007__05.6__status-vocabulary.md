## 05.6 Status vocabulary

Status is not evidence. Status is the final ledger projection of theorem/cert/external comparison protocol ownership.

- `CORE-*`: Foundational detector, spectral, and action theorems.
- `CERT-CLOSED`: Executable finite certificates verifying frozen core invariants.
- `NO-GO`: Formal boundary blocking illegal promotion shortcuts.
- `BRIDGE-CLOSED`: External dictionary mapping frozen core objects.
- `EMPIRICAL-PASSPORT`: External-data comparison with manifest and scheme.
- `PASSPORT-CLOSED`: A bridge whose D0-internal object is frozen and whose external side is only packaging (the seven conditions of `D0-BRIDGE-COMPRESSION-001` below); a closed external dictionary/comparison, mapped to the `EMPIRICAL_PASSPORT` firewall status and therefore **never** promotable to `CORE-THE`.
- `INACTIVE-BRIDGE`: A bridge with no frozen internal object or no deterministic PASS/FAIL protocol — not an active theorem debt; it is removed from the open-joints ledger, not left as an `OPEN` row.
- `CONJECTURE` / `RETIRED`: Unverified statements or obsolete scaffolding.
- `PROOF-TARGET (cert obligation open)`: A forcing claim whose *selection* obligation is named and finite but whose *calculation* cert is not yet registered. This is the only honest label for a still-open joint — it is never silently upgraded, and it is never backed by a fabricated cert token [^b05-13].
- `MECH-LIMIT` (prose status qualifier): a result whose *mechanism* is established and finite-certified, but which is held **below** `THE` because a **limit or external-mechanism** step remains (a named gap) — a conditional result. Used in prose as "status `MECH-LIMIT`, not `THE`" (e.g. the Feshbach generative-dynamics continuum step `D0-GENERATIVE-DYNAMICS-001`; the time↔flavor single-modular-object identification `D0-MODULAR-TIME-FLAVOR-001`). **Disambiguation:** `MECH-LIMIT` is **distinct** from `LEM` = **Lemma** in the standard `DEF/LEM/THE` proof-object taxonomy; the qualifier carries no relation to the Law of Excluded Middle. `MECH-LIMIT` is a prose qualifier, **not** a canonical `release_status` enum value (the carrying claim's release_status stays `CERT-CLOSED`/`BRIDGE-*` with its named gap).

**`D0-BRIDGE-COMPRESSION-001` — bridge closure as passport closure.** A bridge is *not* an indefinite theorem debt. Once the D0-internal object it rests on is **frozen** (a real theorem / certificate / no-go / explicit-assumption owner), the external translation is *packaging*, not a source of structure — exactly the BOOK_00 contract order *standard object → finite D0 operator → theorem/sector law → bridge/passport boundary*. Such a bridge closes as **`PASSPORT-CLOSED`** (firewall status `EMPIRICAL_PASSPORT`; **never** `CORE-THE`) iff it has all seven: (1) frozen internal object; (2) a theorem/cert/no-go/assumption owner; (3) an explicit D0→external translation map; (4) no reverse selection — external data or classical terminology cannot choose the D0 object; (5) a named observable tuple; (6) a deterministic PASS/FAIL rule; (7) negative controls. A bridge missing an internal owner stays **`PROOF-TARGET`**; a bridge with no frozen object or no PASS/FAIL protocol is **`INACTIVE-BRIDGE`** (off the active-debt ledger). Every bridge therefore resolves to one of `PASSPORT-CLOSED` / `PROOF-TARGET` / `INACTIVE-BRIDGE` — no bridge is an indefinite open theorem. `PASSPORT-CLOSED ≠ CORE-THE`: it freezes the external dictionary/comparison so the corpus carries no further mathematical debt for it, while the external theorem (the smooth-manifold limit, the C\*-crossed-product unique trace, an empirical confirmation) remains its own external owner.
