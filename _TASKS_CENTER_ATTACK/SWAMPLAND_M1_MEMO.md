## Claim X (DEF-0.2.2 form)

An exact, ungauged, unbroken global symmetry is M1-inadmissible. Instantiation of the schema with
θ = the conserved global charge Q:

- **(i) assume ¬X:** the theory carries an exact global symmetry with charge Q;
- **(ii) θ is not interior-derived:** with no gauge field there is no Gauss law — Q's value on a
  state is not computable from any interior/boundary protocol of the theory's own dynamics; the
  superselection labels form a standing table (DEF 0.3.1 clause 1);
- **(iii) θ affects distinguishable outcomes:** selection rules forbid otherwise-allowed processes —
  the charge table changes the {0,1} content of readouts (clause 2);
- **(iv) θ is not protocol-unavoidable:** Q commutes with every bounded-region measurement — it is
  invisible to every distinguishability protocol while remaining mandatory bookkeeping (clause 3);
- **(v)** hence Q is an obligatory external catalogue, ⊥M1; ¬X impossible. ∎ (draft)

**The reductio's two escapes are exactly the physics resolutions:** *gauge it* (a Gauss law makes Q
interior-derived through boundary flux — clause (ii) fails, the catalogue becomes protocol) or
*break it* (no exact selection rules — clause (iii) fails). M1 does not merely ban; it forces the
gauge-or-break dichotomy that quantum gravity is observed to enforce.
